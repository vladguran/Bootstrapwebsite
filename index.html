Industrial Roboter

An industrial robot is a universal, programmable machine for handling, assembling or processing workpieces. The industrial robot consists of three components: the manipulator (robot arm), the controller and an effector (tool, gripper, etc.). A sensor is a technical component that can detect certain physical or chemical properties in its surrounding. Once the industrial robot is programmed, the machine is able to carry out a work process autonomously.

Robotertypes

Articulated robots:

These robots are the most common ones and look like a human arm. They are also called robotic arm or manipulator arm. Because of the several degrees of freedom, they can rotate and change into nearly every angle.

Cartesian coordinate robots:

Cartesian robots, also called rectilinear, gantry robots or x-y-z robots have three prismatic joints so every Axles can move. The robot can also rotate at will in every angle like the articulated robot. For a 3D working cartesian robot you need 6 Axles, a 2D working robot needs only 3 Axles to move properly.

Cylindrical coordinate robots:

The cylindrical coordinate robots have a main rotating point and at least one prismatic joint connecting its links. These kinds of robots are effective in tiny spaces.

Spherical coordinate robots:

These types of robots have only rotary joints. These robots were one of the first robots ever used as industrial robots.

Programming

What do you need to know?

To create a program that works on robots, there is need for two types of programming types. You can also use just one of them, usually both of them are needed. These are called Offline and Online Programming.

Input and Output Devices

The complex programming environments have the possibility of installing more tools. You can install a camera or more input devices like a mouse or a keyboard. With the aid of a camera robots could build more robots. They recognize the pieces and build robots.

Structure

Control:

It monitors and directs the movement of the IR. This requires programming.

Drive:

It moves the arms of the kinematic chain and consists of a motor, gears and controls. The drive can be electric, hydraulic or pneumatic.

Sensor technology:

It provides information about the position of the kinematic chain. It is used by the controller to compare the target and actual position.

Gripping systems:

A gripping system creates the connection between the workpiece and the industrial robot.

Kinematics:

It represents the physical realization of the load-bearing structure and creates the spatial assignment between the tool/workpiece and the production facility. It consists of rotary and translatory axes.

Manipulator

The manipulator or robotic arm is a multifunctional manipulator consisting of a series of rigid limbs connected to each other by rotary or sliding joints,

which joints can be adjusted by drives. One end of this link chain is the base, while the other end is free to move and is fitted with a tool or gripper for

performing production work.
